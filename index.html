01 / The thesis

When you hear hoofbeats, think horses — not zebras.

The first aphorism every physician learns. A warning, drilled into trainees on morning rounds, against over-diagnosing the rare.

This show is about the zebras.

The physicians who did the uncommon thing — who took their training past the exam room and into the rooms where capital moves, companies build, policy forms, and the public listens. Rare, by any measure. Worth finding.

02 / Who you'll hear from

Five arenas.
One category.

Physicians who took their training beyond the exam room — into the rooms where the future of medicine is actually built.

Capital

Physicians deploying capital at scale.

Hedge funds. Private equity. Venture. The MDs whose clinical judgment now shapes where the next generation of healthcare investment goes.

Companies

Founders and operators.

The physicians who built the institutions medicine runs on — care delivery platforms, diagnostic companies, digital health, biotech.

Policy

Physicians shaping the rules.

Regulators, public health leaders, and the physicians who write the laws and frameworks the rest of the field lives under.

Public life

Voices that reach beyond medicine.

The doctors shaping how the public understands its own health — through writing, broadcasting, and platforms larger than any single clinic.

Academia × Letters

Physician-writers and thinkers.

The doctors whose work moves the field's self-understanding — the research, the books, the essays that change how medicine talks about itself.

03 / The editorial

What this show does.
And what it refuses.

Three rules. Every episode tests against them.

01.

Goes past the résumé.

Skip the CV recitation. We've read it. We are here to find out how the guest actually thinks — the reasoning behind the decisions that shaped a career.

02.

No hot takes. No hype.

The show is quiet. It earns attention through substance. No political combat. No outrage bait. Real talk about real work.

03.

Honors the oath.

"First, do no harm" is a real tension when the patient is a market, a policy, or a country. We ask how guests navigate it — without looking away.
